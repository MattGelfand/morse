Sportscenter App

UX Design / Onboarding

A Fresh Tilled Soil pet project that began as a redesign but pivoted into an onboarding experience as a result of user testing. FTS doesn't work with many sports-affiliated clients, so it was refreshing to take a stab at something produced by the Worldwide Leader.

View the prototype

Overview

One of the benefits of working at Fresh Tilled Soil is that, in addition to client work, employees are encouraged to work on "passion projects" in their free time. For me, my background as an athlete and general affinity for sports led me to an opportunity to take the essential design and UX skills gained during my apprenticeship and apply them to ESPN's "SportsCenter" app.

▼

Pretty, but flawed

Despite being an avid sports fan, it oddly never occured to me to download ESPN's wildly popular "SportsCenter" app, which provides sports scores, news coverage, videos...basically it's like watching SportsCenter on your phone. And to my dismay, the app itself didn't present any glaring aesthetic flaws. However, I carried on user-testing the app on my fellow employees, asking them to complete six seemingly basic tasks (such as choosing their favorite teams or finding news about a specific sport). These proved to be far more challenging than anticipated, regardless of whether the user followed sports or not. By the end, it became clear that while app didn't need a redesign, it definitely need better instructions.

Final Product

After experimenting with a variety of different onboarding patterns (annotated, embedded and dedicated - read more about these patterns here), I landed on a hybrid. Users are initially prompted to take action (compiling a list of favorite teams) before signing up, and are given a blank state that they can fill in. After signing up, they're provided with a quick tour how each of the pain points discovered during user testing works, and then they're off to explore.
