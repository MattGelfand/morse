Process

User-focused design means never making assumptions. In order to understand the bigger picture - how a site or app functions, if it's pleasant to use, if the user can accomplish goals with ease - you need to incorporate user feedback into the design process. And that process involves more than simply asking people a few questions; It's properly running usability tests, forming a hypothesis, creating an effective information architecture and devising prototypes to test those assumptions...then iteratating on those results.

About

I devise, design and occasionally develop smart and accessibile user experiences. I own a master's degree in interactive publishing from Northwestern's Medill School, and currently work as UX Designer at Fresh Tilled Soil, a firm that's been a pioneer of the user experience discipline for more than a decade. I believe in creating products based on user's needs, behaviors and habits, and not being afraid to build (and fail) early, because the best ideas are never your first. Other mantras: Steal like an artist, don't make users think, and always have a plan - without one you have no destination, which means you can never arrive.
