Overview

One of my first design challenges as an apprentice at Fresh Tilled Soil was to take a broad topic and create a prototype of an app that does one specific thing within that over-arching theme. My topic, news, seemed easy enough, especially given my background in journalism. However, uncovering a specific and novel idea turned out to be more challenging than I imagined.

▼

Process

Instinctively, the concept of time (or news readers' lack of it) seemed like an obvious topic to tackle. But the "summarize-your-news" market - with such apps like Reverb, Pocket, Feedly, Circa and Yahoo News Digest - already had that niche covered. If I were to create something game-changing, focusing on time was out of the question.

I shifted to the idea of news-sharing on social media, and what it means to be a thought-leader on the web. I sent out surveys to potential users via Google Forms to gauge why people share news, the importance of their web reputation, and what their motivations were to curate news for friends via social media outlets. Overwhelmingly, respondents expressed altruism - sharing for the benefit of others without receiving anything in return - as a primary driver. Recognition for sharing in the form of comments or Likes were also deemed important.

I took this information and devised a rough prototype of an app that provides a percentage of liklihood that the post you're sharing will be shared or Liked, based on factors such as time and day of the post, the post's mood and whether your friends' interests match the topic of the post. By providing a unique percentage to each potential post, users benefit from the "slot machine" effect, which encourages repeat usage.

Revisited

Given the limited timeframe of the initial challenge (two weeks), I was unable to truly flesh out the app further. I revisited the idea three months later as a side-project, and, based on my previous results and further research from scientific journals, I've expanded on the concept of knowledge sharing in the form of chatrooms. In the vein of the AOL chats of yesteryear, users would organically read and share a story they found interesting, and the app would suggest other users (friends on Facebook) with known similar interests. The user could then choose other users they'd like to engage in a discussion with, and invite them into a chatroom specific to the story they shared. Sketches, flows and wireframes to be posted shortly.
