Backstory

Concept / Strategy / Design / Dev

An app prototype made over the course of two days at a hackathon in Indianapolis held by the Society for News Design. I'm particularly proud of the quality and utility the prototype possesses given our limited creative window.

See it live

Overview

SND Hackathons breaks its participants into teams and primes them by asking the question "How Might We..." then sets them off on a two-day journey to create something useful. My team of five set out to answer how we could better help readers understand stories by improving their context.

▼

How Might We...

Our idea stemmed from the notion that each news is story is essentially a collection of individual updates, and each story presented on a site or app's homepage is just an entry point into a larger narrative. Readers may occasionally lose that context, forcing them to Google through a maze of old news, playing catch-up. Backstory elimates this headache by allowing users to engage in a story from start-to-finish, all on a single page.

Using Hackdash, we documented our brainstorming session, explorning certain concepts such as allowing users to add their thoughts to stories as to what they think might happen next, or a karma/upvoting system. We eventually fleshed out an information architecture with a home screen and navigation. Color choices and typefaces soon followed.

Final Product

Tight deadlines tend to squeeze the best ideas out of people, and that certainly turned out to be the case here. Given more time, Backstory could easily become real-world product, with additional features such as the ability for users to log-in and subscribe to specific stories that mattered to them, or see what their friends are tracking. The app looks nice, contains all the functionality mentioned in our IA and is responsive. Not bad for 48 hours. You can find a more detailed breakdown of the ideation and design process here.
