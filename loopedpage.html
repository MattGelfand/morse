looped-in

Mobile UI Design / Strategy

A Fresh Tilled Soil client, looped-in came to FTS with a goal of becoming the "go-to" mobile payment option for college students, essentially replacing the widely-used method of loading money onto college IDs.

View the prototype

Overview

Looped-in's goal of becoming a ubiquitous mobile payment option for college students was hindered by two primary issues: a lack of a trustworthy, iOS standard interface, and barriers to widespread adoption from school administrators, merchants and students. Our team set out to better understand the company's mission and the lay the aesthetic and structural foundations to increase the their chances of success down the line.

▼

Process

━

Essential to the design process is addressing many of the assumptions both we as designers and the company itself have about who is using their product and, if it's already an existing product, why they think it needs improvement. During deep dive sessions as Fresh Tilled Soil, we dig deeper into user motivations, flows, journeys and try to understand what success looks like to the company and its stakeholders.

Final Product

━

Following the deep dive, the actual design process consisted of three stages: First, an initial stage of "quick wins" where we dove into the app's existing code and altered the interface to become more user-friendly. Next, we sketched, fleshed out information architecture and wireframes based on up-to-date iOS 7 standards. Finally, we created a high-fidelity, clickable prototype made in prototyping app InVision to send off to a team of back-end developers.

The result was a cleaner, more straighforward interface, with understated (but understandable) icons, a more trustworthy typeface and color scheme and a consolidation of all the essential user flows in a "super button," allowing the user to access the app's primary features at any point.
