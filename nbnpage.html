North By Northwestern

Concept / Strategy / Design

The product of a four-month design fellowship at Northwestern's Knight Lab, a software lab that creates apps for journalists. Completed a total of three high-fidelity prototypes for a redesign of Northwestern's online magazine.

See it live

Overview

━

North by Northwestern is a daily online magazine started by the students at the Medill School of Journalism, and it needed a facelift. I had worked with the Knight Lab's designers and developers on various projects for six months prior, but for this assignment I'd be working solo. There were no guarantees the magazine would implement my designs when it was all said and done, but I was excited to take a stab and use the power of user testing and feedback to curate a worthwhile alternative.

▼

Iterate...and repeat

━

Prior to writing a single line of code - or even putting pen to paper - I sent out Facebook messages to the magazine's editors and offered a call-to-action to its readers to provide me with feedback of the website. Additional research included using a heatmapping service to discover which sections users were clicking on most frequently (so they could be more prominent) and analyzing other popular news publications the editors felt they'd like their site to emulate - The Verge, The Atlantic, Pitchfork and The New York Times all made the cut.

I set up weekly meetings with the site's editor to discuss progress and run layout and typeface options by her. I would then take her feedback, iterate, and run those ideas by the lab's chief of staff, a former designer at the Boston Globe. I continued this process for the duration of the fellowship, completing designs for both the site's homepage and article page.

Final Product

━

The final concept featured a more modern, sleek look that included larger, scalable images, the addition of a (previously sparsely-used) orange color more prominently in the color scheme and a professional-yet-playful paragraph and headline typeface combination (Calluna and Museo Sans Condensed). Leaving the fellowship for a new venture meant I couldn't follow-through and push for actual implementation of the designs, but it turned out to be a valuable experience nonetheless.
